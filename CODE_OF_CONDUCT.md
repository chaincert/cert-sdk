# CertID Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the CertID open-source ecosystem a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community, prioritizing the advancement of secure, hardware-anchored identity infrastructure.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:
- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other contributors.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback, especially regarding cryptographic implementations and security audits.
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals but for the overall CertID ecosystem and its users.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information such as a physical or email address without their explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or institutional setting.

## Security and Responsible Disclosure

Because CertID operates critical Web3 infrastructure and handles identity attestation,
we hold our community to the highest standards of security ethics. Publicly attacking the project over a vulnerability,
or maliciously exploiting bugs on mainnet or testnet environments,
vioates this Code of Conduct. All security vulnerabilities must be reported privately and responsibly to the core team.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all CertID community spaces (including GitHub repositories,
official Telegram channels,
and Discord servers),
and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples include using an official email address,
publishing via an official social media account,
or acting as an appointed representative at online or offline events (such as hackathons or Demo Day).

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive,
harassing,
or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at [admin@c3rt.org](mailto:admin@c3rt.org).

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining consequences for violations:
1. **Correction**
   - *Community Impact:* Use of inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior.
   - *Consequence:* A private written warning explaining why the behavior was inappropriate.
2. **Warning**
   - *Community Impact:* Violation through a single incident or series of actions.
   - *Consequence:* A warning with consequences for continued behavior; no interaction with involved parties including enforcement personnel for a specified period.
3. **Temporary Ban**
   - *Community Impact:* Serious violation including sustained inappropriate behavior.
   - *Consequence:* Temporary ban from interactions or public communication for a specified period.
4. **Permanent Ban**
   - *Community Impact:* Patterned violations including harassment or disparagements.
   - *Consequence:* Permanent ban from all public interactions within CertID.
